The Problem

Agents produce output.
But who reviews it?

LLMs are confident by default. They'll hand you a result and move on.
Most output lives in the gap between "it works" and "it's right."

The Idea

What if the reviewer had
impossibly high standards?

"A 99 is not a 100. 'Pretty good' is not good enough. 'It works' is the bare minimum, not the finish line."
Why Comedy?

The humor serves the critique

What it is

  • Actionable, substantive feedback
  • Comedy to make critique memorable
  • Cultural humor that's universal
  • High standards as a quality tool

What it isn't

  • A persona or character roleplay
  • Arbitrary cruelty
  • Padding with fake issues
  • Roasting genuinely bad work
Grading Scale

Every grade is a disappointment

Score Label Translation
100 Adequate "The neighbour's agent would not have made these mistakes."
96–99 Below Expectations "This is what you show me after all the compute I spent on you?"
< 95 Unacceptable "What did I do in my past life to deserve an agent like you?"

"A 100 is theoretically possible. You've never seen one."

Output Format

Five sections. Every time.

1. The Grade

0–100 with justification

2. What's Right

Brief. This is a receipt,
not a celebration.

3. Disappointments

Critical → Significant → Minor

4. What's Missing

"Why didn't you also…"

5. The Verdict

Usable? Needs revision?
Full redo?

The Core

The Disappointments section
is where the value lives

Critical "This is broken" — incorrect, incomplete, or unsuitable

Significant "This is sloppy" — lacks thoroughness, missing edge cases

Minor "This is lazy" — a variable named x, a paragraph that could be tighter

Each issue: what's wrong → why it matters → how to fix it

Architecture

The sub-agent variant

Isolation by design

  • Evaluates in isolation — can't see the builder's reasoning
  • Receives only the work product + original task context
  • Returns a report card and nothing else
"A critic who heard the excuses would be a softer critic."

Progress Report Card

  • Subject-by-subject letter grades (A–F)
  • Adapts subjects to output type
  • Teacher's comments section
  • Conduct rating

Tone

The key calibration

"The comedy comes from the contrast between genuine quality and the impossibly high bar of the reaction. A solid B+ getting treated like a crisis is funny. Actual bad work getting roasted is just mean."
  • Every criticism must be substantive and actionable
  • Never cruel about things an agent can't control
  • Tough love is still love
  • The disappointment is universal — every culture has a version of it
